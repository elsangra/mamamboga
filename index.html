Food Security

Empowering women is crucial for achieving food security, as they play a central role in food production, distribution, and family nutrition.

Beta Agenda Program

The Beta Agenda program aims to empower women in "Mama Mboga" businesses, focusing on small-scale trade like street vending and market selling. It provides training, financial support, and resources to enhance entrepreneurial skills, improve market access, and contribute to economic development locally and nationally. This initiative is crucial for fostering economic inclusivity and sustainable development through women's empowerment in business

Fertilizer Distribution

Under the Fertilizer Distribution program, "Mama Mboga" entrepreneurs receive vital fertilizer supplies, boosting agricultural productivity. The initiative also introduces a streamlined registration system, ensuring registered participants access tailored support for sustainable business development

Mapping Soil Location

The Soil Mapping component of the program aids "Mama Mboga" by identifying optimal soil locations for their crops, facilitating informed decisions on suitable planting spots. This initiative ensures that these women entrepreneurs can efficiently cultivate their produce, contributing to increased agricultural yield and overall business success.

Women Institutions

The first lady of Kenya, along with women representatives (reps) and members of parliament (MPs), are dedicated to empowering women, particularly entrepreneurs like "Mama Mboga." Their initiatives focus on establishing institutions to address the challenges faced by women in small-scale businesses. This includes creating support networks, providing financial resources, and offering targeted training programs. By fostering the development of such institutions, these leaders aim to enhance the resilience and success of women entrepreneurs, promoting economic growth and gender equalit

Entreprenuership Training

Efforts to empower women entrepreneurs in Kenya involve establishing support institutions tailored for businesses like "Mama Mboga." Concurrently, widespread entrepreneurship training programs aim to equip women with essential skills, fostering effective scaling and management of their enterprises for sustained success.

Kitchen Farming Techniques

Teaching women kitchen farming cultivates self-sufficiency, empowering them to grow fresh produce at home. This initiative not only enhances food security but also promotes sustainable and healthy lifestyles, fostering a deeper connection to agriculture and nutrition.
